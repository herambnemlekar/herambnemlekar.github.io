Bio

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Interactive and Collaborative Autonomous Robotics Lab at the University of Southern California (USC), advised by Dr. Stefanos Nikolaidis. Broadly, my research involves studying human behaviour to develop machine learning approaches for improving human-robot interaction (HRI). Specifically, I focus on enabling robots to quickly adapt to the preferences of a human teammate and assist them in complex tasks to reduce human effort and improve the team efficiency.

In my Ph.D. thesis, I propose a framework for efficiently learning human preferences in actual assembly tasks wihout requiring explicit demonstrations (that are often tedious and time-consuming) by exploiting (i) similarities between preferences of different humans, and (ii) similarities between different tasks.

Currently, I am seeking post-doctoral research positions for 2023!
