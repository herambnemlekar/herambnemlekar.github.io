Bio

I am motivated by the possibility of robots coexisting with humans at comparable intelligence levels, and it is a dream that I constantly strive towards. Accordingly, my research interests are focused on robot learning and planning for effective Human-Robot Interaction (HRI).

Currently, I am a PhD Student in the Interactive and Collaborative Autonomous Robotics Lab at the University of Southern California (USC), advised by Dr. Stefanos Nikolaidis. My research focuses on developing computational approaches for - learning and inferring human behaviour from demonstrations, and leveraging it to inform robot decisions for effective human-robot collaboration.

Apart from HRI, I am also fascinated by space exploration and interstellar travel. Given my background in robotics, I hope to (at some point in my life) apply the concepts of robot estimation, planning and learning for - planetary exploration and even HRI in space operations.
