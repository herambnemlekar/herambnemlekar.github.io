Bio

I am motivated by the possibility of robots coexisting with humans to support and enhance human capabilities in various domains ranging from industrial assemblies to activities of daily living. As robots become more 'human-safe', physical human-robot interaction will be ineveitable. While industrial automation has helped to maximized gains on a system level, on an individual level, what signifies a ‘gain’ can be different for different people. Therefore, my research focuses on improving the overall performance (e.g., task efficiency) as well as the individual human objectives (e.g., user satisfaction) in human-robot interaction.

Currently, I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Interactive and Collaborative Autonomous Robotics Lab at the University of Southern California (USC), advised by Dr. Stefanos Nikolaidis. My research focuses on developing computational approaches for - efficiently learning and inferring human preferences from demonstrations, and leveraging it to inform robot decisions for effective human-robot collaboration.

Apart from HRI, I am also fascinated by space exploration. Building on my background in robotics, I hope to (at some point in my life) apply the concepts of robot estimation, planning and learning for - planetary exploration and even HRI in space operations.
