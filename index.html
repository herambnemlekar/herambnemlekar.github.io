Bio

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Southern California (USC) under the guidance of Stefanos Nikolaidis and Satyandra K. Gupta, and M.S. in Robotics Engineering from the Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) under Zhi (Jane) Li.

My research focuses on enabling robots to quickly adapt to their human partners for assisting them in complex tasks; reducing human effort, increasing task efficiency, and improving user experience. Accordingly, my work lies at the intersection of robotics, machine learning, and human modeling.

In my Ph.D. thesis, I propose methods for efficiently learning human preferences without needing costly human demonstrations by leveraging similarities between different humans and different tasks.
